06 / 16
Call 2 · Free

The Closer — not a sale, a prescription.

A dedicated role with both sales acumen and clinical depth. Walks the family through the ATEC results and prescribes the right tier. The frame is therapeutic, not transactional.

The Closer's superpower

Hybrid skill set — fluent enough in the clinical to interpret the ATEC and explain the protocol, persuasive enough to handle objections, structured enough to close. One person, two domains. This role does not exist on most healthcare teams — it's a competitive advantage.

What happens on the call

Review ATEC results out loud. Translate the score into what it means for the child's daily life. Explain which tier matches that profile. Walk through what the next 12 weeks look like. Answer real clinical questions in real time.

The script frame

"Based on Sarah's ATEC results and what you've told us, here's the protocol our clinical team is recommending — and exactly why."

The frame removes the transaction. The Closer is not a salesperson upselling — they're a clinician's proxy translating data into a recommendation. Families enroll because they trust the diagnosis, not because they were persuaded.

Open Item

Closer hiring profile + comp model

Hybrid sales/clinical role is rare. Likely a former case manager, RN, or BCBA with sales aptitude. Comp structure (base + close commission) needs design before first hire.

After the Intensive · Continuing Care

A two-year journey, not a 12-week sprint.

The 12-week intensive is exactly that — intensive. It generates the early wins families need to believe the protocol works. But Dr. Kyle is clear: this is a two-year journey. The next 21 months are where the gains get locked in, neural adaptation compounds, and the family transitions from acute-care intensity to a sustainable maintenance rhythm.

What Sarah and Ethan see — Continuing Care view

After the Wk 12 graduation, the family's experience tapers but doesn't end. Same care team, same protocol, lighter cadence. Below is the actual Continuing Care dashboard the Martin family logs into starting in month 4.

app.neurohomesolutions.com / family / continuing-care

Active involvement tapers — but never stops

Months 1–6 — Stabilization

Weekly OT/PT sessions still active. Daily Hub exercises. Concierge coaching weekly. Quarterly ATEC. Goal: lock in the intensive's gains while neural adaptation is still high-velocity.

Months 7–12 — Independence

OT/PT shifts to bi-weekly. Hub usage becomes family-led. Concierge moves to bi-weekly check-ins. Quarterly ATEC. Focus moves from acute gains to functional milestones at school and home.

Months 13–24 — Maintenance

OT/PT monthly for laser supervision and protocol updates. Hub is self-service. Concierge optional. Annual ATEC review. The family runs the day-to-day; the team stays accessible.

Why OT/PT involvement never fully drops out

The Class 3B laser stays in the home throughout continuing care — and a Class 3B device requires a trained clinician to actively supervise administration. That clinical touchpoint is not optional, which is why every continuing care tier carries embedded OT/PT hours and laser lease economics. It's also what protects clinical outcomes against the natural drift that happens when families fall back to self-directed protocols.

Compliance · Multi-State Scale

The four gates that are the moat.

Every growth vector runs through a compliance gate, not around one. The same architecture that keeps the business defensible is the architecture that lets us turn on a new state in weeks, not months. This is why scope-of-practice work, claims-language discipline, and compact-licensure mechanics are foundational, not finishing touches.

Four gates, one architecture

🔒

Licensing Gate

State-by-state rollout based on confirmed clinician licensing coverage. No state goes live without a scope-of-practice opinion. Patient location is verified at every session — hard stop if out-of-state.

📡

Telehealth Gate

Cross-state practice runs through interstate compacts and state-specific telehealth registrations. Tight scope-of-practice boundaries for remote supervision. MD oversight is the architecture, not Kyle's individual license.

📝

Claims Gate

Disciplined, audited language across every patient-facing surface: support, function, engagement, family coaching, adjunctive. Never treat, cure, remediate. Claims-language audit is a release-blocker on every campaign.

🛡️

Device Gate

LED panels under FDA General Wellness exemption. Class 3B laser deployment requires Rx, training, signed safety waiver, contraindication screening, and live OT/PT supervision. Documented stop rules at the family level.

Dr. Kyle is the Protocol Architect — he doesn't see patients in this model. Patient-facing care flows through OTs and PTs (under MD supervision), and they reach new states via interstate compacts. The numbers below are why the model is national from Day 1.

OTEIM (single California clinic, no visible interstate licensing infrastructure) and the broader in-clinic intensive market are geographically capped.
